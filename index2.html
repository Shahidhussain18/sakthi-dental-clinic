Frequently Asked Questions

Can medication completely relieve tooth pain?

Not entirely. While medications may offer short-term relief, long-lasting relief requires identifying and treating the root cause.

Does teeth scaling cause enamel damage, sensitivity, or gaps between teeth?

No. Scaling safely removes plaque and tartar without damaging enamel or creating gaps.

Can fluorosis stains be removed through scaling?

Scaling can remove surface stains but not fluorosis, which may require bleaching or veneers.

Can painful teeth be treated with fillings?

Not always. If the cavity reaches the pulp, root canal treatment or extraction may be necessary.

Is placing a cap or crown necessary after root canal treatment?

Yes. A crown protects the treated tooth, preventing fractures and adding durability.

Can milk teeth need root canal treatment?

Yes, if a milk tooth has a deep cavity, an RCT can preserve it and prevent infection.

How can I maintain good oral hygiene?

Brush twice daily, rinse after meals, floss regularly, and schedule routine check-ups.

What is the best age to get braces?

Braces are most effective between ages 12 and 14, but earlier intervention may be needed.

Do all wisdom teeth need to be removed?

No, only if they are impacted, infected, or causing pain or crowding.

Is it important to replace missing teeth after extraction?

Yes, to maintain proper bite alignment and prevent adjacent teeth from shifting.

What are the benefits of dental implants?

Implants restore appearance and functionality, improving confidence and quality of life.

Which type of toothbrush and toothpaste should I use?

Use a soft or medium-bristled toothbrush and non-abrasive fluoride toothpaste.

What causes tooth sensitivity?

Sensitivity can result from enamel wear, decay, exposed roots, or hairline cracks.

How often should I see a dentist?

Visit every six months for routine check-ups and professional cleaning.
